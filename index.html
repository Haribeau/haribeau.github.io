About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Taniti Declares Official National Sport

In a surprising move, Taniti’s government has announced a new official national sport: Competitive Napping. Officials believe this bold new approach will boost productivity and the health of the population, promoting rest as essential to the nation’s well-being.

Taniti's Volcano Suddenly Declares Itself a Sovereign Nation

A local volcano on Taniti's southern coast erupts with a message declaring its independence, demanding international recognition and a seat at the UN.

Taniti to Launch World’s First Solar-Powered Ice Cream Truck

In a sweet twist on sustainability, Taniti is set to debut the world’s first solar-powered ice cream truck. The truck, which runs entirely on solar energy, will serve eco-friendly ice cream made from locally sourced ingredients.

5 Dec

Taniti Annual Coconut Festival

The island's most anticipated celebration of the coconut harvest, featuring local crafts, coconut-inspired foods, and traditional dances. The festival includes a coconut tree climbing competition, a coconut cooking contest, and live music performances by local artists. Visitors can enjoy coconut-themed drinks and desserts while learning about Taniti's agricultural heritage.

13 Dec

Volcano Trek Adventure

An exciting guided hiking expedition to the top of Taniti's active volcano. The trek offers breathtaking views of the island's volcanic landscape, lush rainforests, and nearby beaches. A special presentation by local geologists will explain the island's geology, and adventurers will experience the thrill of standing near the crater while safely observing volcanic activity.

7 Jan

Ocean Conservation Symposium

A global gathering of environmentalists, scientists, and conservationists dedicated to preserving Taniti's coral reefs and marine ecosystems. The symposium will include lectures, workshops, and discussions on the impact of climate change, sustainable fishing practices, and marine biodiversity. Local students and activists will also present their own environmental projects aimed at protecting the island's oceans.

20 Jan

International Sailing Regatta

An exhilarating sailing race where international sailors compete in various categories, including traditional island boats and modern yachts. The regatta brings together sailors from across the Pacific for a week of intense competition, social events, and cultural exchange. Spectators can watch the thrilling races along the coast while enjoying food stalls and live performances from Taniti’s top musicians.
